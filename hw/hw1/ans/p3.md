# Problem 3 — Baseline for Variance Reduction

We have one non-terminal state $s$ and three actions $a,b,c$ with rewards

$$
r(s,a)=100,\quad r(s,b)=99,\quad r(s,c)=98.
$$

The policy is softmax with parameters

$$
\theta_a=0,\quad \theta_b=\ln 3,\quad \theta_c=\ln 2,
$$

so

$$
\pi(a|s)=\frac{1}{6},\qquad
\pi(b|s)=\frac{1}{2},\qquad
\pi(c|s)=\frac{1}{3}.
$$

Because the episode terminates after one action, the policy-gradient sample is

$$
\hat g = r(s,a_0)\nabla_\theta \log \pi(a_0|s).
$$

For softmax,

$$
\nabla_\theta \log \pi(a_i|s) = e_i - p,
$$

where $p = (1/6,1/2,1/3)^\top$ and $e_i$ is the one-hot basis vector.

---

## 3(a) Mean and covariance of $\hat g$

### Sample gradients

- If $a_0=a$:

$$
\hat g_a = 100
\begin{bmatrix}
5/6\\ -1/2\\ -1/3
\end{bmatrix}
=
\begin{bmatrix}
250/3\\ -50\\ -100/3
\end{bmatrix}
$$

- If $a_0=b$:

$$
\hat g_b = 99
\begin{bmatrix}
-1/6\\ 1/2\\ -1/3
\end{bmatrix}
=
\begin{bmatrix}
-33/2\\ 99/2\\ -33
\end{bmatrix}
$$

- If $a_0=c$:

$$
\hat g_c = 98
\begin{bmatrix}
-1/6\\ -1/2\\ 2/3
\end{bmatrix}
=
\begin{bmatrix}
-49/3\\ -49\\ 196/3
\end{bmatrix}
$$

### Expected gradient

The expected reward is

$$
V^{\pi_\theta}(s)
= \frac{1}{6}\cdot 100 + \frac{1}{2}\cdot 99 + \frac{1}{3}\cdot 98
= \frac{593}{6}.
$$

Hence

$$
\mathbb E[\hat g]
=
\begin{bmatrix}
\pi(a)(100-V)\\
\pi(b)(99-V)\\
\pi(c)(98-V)
\end{bmatrix}
=
\begin{bmatrix}
7/36\\
1/12\\
-5/18
\end{bmatrix}.
$$

### Second moment and covariance

The covariance matrix is

$$
\operatorname{Cov}(\hat g)
= \mathbb E[\hat g \hat g^\top] - \mathbb E[\hat g]\mathbb E[\hat g]^\top.
$$

The exact result is

$$
\operatorname{Cov}(\hat g)=
\begin{bmatrix}
\frac{1791617}{1296} & -\frac{361177}{432} & -\frac{354043}{648} \\
-\frac{361177}{432} & \frac{351665}{144} & -\frac{346909}{216} \\
-\frac{354043}{648} & -\frac{346909}{216} & \frac{697385}{324}
\end{bmatrix}.
$$

---

## 3(b) Using the value function as baseline

Take the baseline

$$
B(s) = V^{\pi_\theta}(s) = \frac{593}{6}.
$$

Then the estimator is

$$
\tilde g = \bigl(r(s,a_0)-V^{\pi_\theta}(s)\bigr)\nabla_\theta \log \pi(a_0|s).
$$

The centered rewards are

$$
100-\frac{593}{6}=\frac{7}{6},\quad
99-\frac{593}{6}=\frac{1}{6},\quad
98-\frac{593}{6}=-\frac{5}{6}.
$$

### Expected gradient

The mean is unchanged:

$$
\mathbb E[\tilde g]
=
\begin{bmatrix}
7/36\\
1/12\\
-5/18
\end{bmatrix}.
$$

### Covariance

The covariance becomes

$$
\operatorname{Cov}(\tilde g)=
\begin{bmatrix}
\frac{41}{324} & -\frac{5}{54} & -\frac{11}{324} \\
-\frac{5}{54} & \frac{1}{9} & -\frac{1}{54} \\
-\frac{11}{324} & -\frac{1}{54} & \frac{17}{324}
\end{bmatrix}.
$$

This is much smaller than the no-baseline case, which is exactly the variance-reduction effect of subtracting $V^\pi(s)$.

---

## 3(c) Optimal baseline

For a scalar state-dependent baseline $B(s)$, the variance-minimizing choice minimizes the trace of the covariance matrix. For a single-state problem, the optimal baseline is

$$
B^*(s)
=
\frac{\mathbb E\left[r(s,a)\,\|\nabla_\theta \log \pi(a|s)\|^2\right]}
{\mathbb E\left[\|\nabla_\theta \log \pi(a|s)\|^2\right]}.
$$

For the three actions, the score-function norms are

$$
\|\nabla \log \pi(a|s)\|^2 = \frac{19}{18},\qquad
\|\nabla \log \pi(b|s)\|^2 = \frac{7}{18},\qquad
\|\nabla \log \pi(c|s)\|^2 = \frac{13}{18}.
$$

Thus

$$
B^*(s)
=
\frac{
\frac{1}{6}\cdot 100\cdot \frac{19}{18}
+ \frac{1}{2}\cdot 99\cdot \frac{7}{18}
+ \frac{1}{3}\cdot 98\cdot \frac{13}{18}
}{
\frac{1}{6}\cdot \frac{19}{18}
+ \frac{1}{2}\cdot \frac{7}{18}
+ \frac{1}{3}\cdot \frac{13}{18}
}
= \frac{6527}{66}.
$$

So the optimal baseline is

$$
\boxed{B^*(s)=\frac{6527}{66}\approx 98.8939.}
$$

This is slightly larger than $V^{\pi_\theta}(s)=593/6\approx 98.8333$, because the trace-minimizing baseline weights actions by the squared norm of the policy score function.
